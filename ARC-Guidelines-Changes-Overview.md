**2026 version:** This was consolidated into a new comprehensive "Temporary Structures" section with expanded provisions.

## New Sections Added

### 1. Definitions Section (Entirely New)
Added clear definitions for key terms:

- **Common Areas:** All property within Reserve at Minneola owned/maintained by Association
- **Distinctive Flora:** Native Florida plants protected by state/local ordinances (native palms, flowering shrubs, specimen trees per Lake County/Minneola ordinances)
- **Like New Condition:** Detailed maintenance standard including:
  - Fences: vertical, stable, clean, no warped/bent panels
  - Play structures: secure anchoring, fresh paint/stain, intact canvas/fabric
  - Painted surfaces: no peeling, fading, discoloration
  - Screening: taut, no tears or holes
- **Substantially Similar:** Materials matching original in appearance, quality, function (even if not identical composition)

### 2. Screened Entry of Home (Renamed & Reorganized)
- Previously titled "Front Entry of Home"
- Moved to be near other screen-related sections

### 3. Hurricane Protection (Comprehensive New Section)
Replaced the limited "Storm/Hurricane Shutters" section with expanded coverage:

**New comprehensive coverage includes:**
- Roof systems meeting ASCE 7-22 standards
- Permanent fixed storm shutters (accordion, colonial, Bahama, panel style)
- Roll-down track storm shutters
- Impact-resistant windows and doors
- Polycarbonate panels and Lexan panels
- Reinforced garage doors
- Erosion controls
- Exterior fixed generators (with Florida House Bill 293 reference)
- Fuel storage tanks for generators

**New specifications added:**
- Color matching requirements for shutters
- Florida Building Code wind resistance requirements
- Detailed timeline for temporary shutter installation/removal (72-hour advance, 14-day removal)
- Impact-resistant window frame color requirements
- Extension request process for storm damage situations

### 4. Temporary Structures (Comprehensive New Section)
Consolidates and expands temporary structure rules:

**Now covers:**
- **Portable Storage/Moving Containers (PODS):**
  - Extended from 7 days to 1 month maximum
  - Must be on driveway only
  - Extension requests allowed
  
- **Construction Dumpsters:**
  - 1 month maximum (new provision)
  - Driveway only
  - Must be covered when not in active use
  
- **Temporary Shelters During Construction:**
  - 1 month maximum
  - Scaffolding and protective coverings permitted
  - Must meet safety codes

**General requirements added:**
- No blocking sidewalks/impeding traffic
- Must be secured against wind
- Homeowner liability for damage
- Written extension requests required

### 5. Florida-Friendly Landscaping (New Subsection)
Added explicit reference to Florida Statute 720.3075:

> Homeowners have the right to install Florida-friendly landscaping. ARC approval is required for all landscaping projects to ensure compliance with Florida Statute requirements and community aesthetic standards.

**Required application elements:**
- Plant selection list demonstrating Florida-friendly plants
- Design plan showing water conservation measures
- FYN certification if applicable

### 6. Solar Panels and Solar Collectors (Consolidated & Expanded)
**2020:** Brief mention under "Gutters and Solar Collectors"
**2026:** Dedicated comprehensive section with:

- Consolidated requirements statement
- Florida Statute 163.04 compliance reference
- Roof-mounted equipment specifications
- Ground-mounted equipment specifications
- Maintenance requirements
- 30-day repair/removal requirement for damaged equipment

**2020 Version:**

### 9. Outbuildings, Sheds and Storage Containers
**2020:** 
> "No temporary outbuildings, sheds, or other outdoor storage containers shall be permitted on any Lots. Exceptions may be approved by the ARC."

**2026:**
> "Sheds and storage containers **visible from streets, adjacent parcels, or common areas** require ARC approval."

**Impact:** Major policy shift - sheds/storage containers now permitted, just need approval if visible.

### 10. Roofs Section (Consolidated)
**2020:** "Roofs" was a single-line section, with "Front Roof Changes" as a separate section
**2026:** "Front Roof Changes" is now a subsection under "Roofs"

### 11. Gas Tanks (Propane and/or Natural)
**Added provision for generator fuel tanks:**
> "**Exception:** Fuel storage tanks installed in conjunction with an exterior fixed generator for hurricane protection purposes are permitted per Florida House Bill 293, subject to applicable building codes and screening requirements."

### 12. Windows - Replacement, Tinting and Treatments
**Added new provision for impact-resistant windows:**
> "**Impact-Resistant Windows and Doors:** Impact-resistant windows and doors that meet Florida Building Code requirements are permitted. Frame colors shall match or be substantially similar to existing frames, or match the home's trim color. All installations must obtain required permits."
